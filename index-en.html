Global Sugar Information & Projections

The global sugar market for 2025/2026 presents challenges and opportunities. Here we highlight the types of sugar, producing regions, and key factors affecting logistics and international trade.

ICUMSA 45

Refined white sugar, high purity and global demand for food and beverages.

ICUMSA 100/150

Standard quality white sugar, ideal for baking and food industries.

VHP 600-1200

High polarization raw sugar, raw material for international refineries.

Main Producing Regions

Brazil

World leader, with Santos and Paranaguá ports for massive exports. Stable harvest outlook and focus on refined sugar.

Projected Production

CONAB estimates a slight decrease in the 2025/2026 harvest, but with potential records in refined sugar production due to a shift in focus from ethanol.

Port Infrastructure

The Port of Santos leads with over 2,000 tons/hour export capacity. Paranaguá remains a key second node for mass exports.

Key Certifications

SGS, Bureau Veritas, and FIESP support the quality and legality of each exported batch. Includes certificates of origin, weight, health, and shipping.

Thailand

Production recovery for 2025/2026. Efficient logistics from key ports such as Laem Chabang and Bangkok.

European Union

Production aimed at domestic consumption, with France and Germany leading in beet sugar.

Expected Production

DG AGRI projects an 8% decrease due to reduced acreage and pests. Beet sugar (ICUMSA 45–150) is mostly for internal consumption.

Main Ports

Antwerp, Hamburg, and Le Havre channel exports with multimodal logistics (trucks, trains, barges).

Key Certifications

Documentation supported by CEFS and the European Commission, includes EUR.1 Certificate of Origin and DUA Administrative Declaration.

Key Market Factors

Climate Volatility

Extreme weather events affect production, altering supply and prices.

Government Policies

Regulations on ethanol and subsidies modify export availability and market orientation.

Freight and Logistics Costs

Port congestion, container availability, and fuel prices impact the final cost.

Global Demand

Emerging markets such as Asia and Africa drive sustained growth of global sugar consumption.
