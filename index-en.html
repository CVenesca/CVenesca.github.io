Global Financial Structure

CVenesca Group LLC: Your Partner in Global Trade

Our operational solidity is based on a network of top-tier banking partners, selected from the world's Top 50. This strategic alliance with financial giants from **America, Europe, and Asia**, along with key partners like **Emirates NBD** in Dubai, allows us to guarantee maximum security, liquidity, and reliability in every transaction. Below, we break down the tools and compliance framework that protect your international trade operations.

Bank Guarantees

Financial instruments that ensure the fulfillment of payment obligations and minimize risk in large-scale commercial transactions.

  • SBLC (Standby Letter of Credit) MT 760: A payment guarantee from the issuing bank that is activated only if the buyer defaults on their obligations. It acts as a safety net against non-compliance.
  • DLC (Documentary Letter of Credit) MT 700: A letter of credit that guarantees payment to the seller once they present the agreed-upon shipping documents. It is the most secure tool for international trade.

Pillars of Security

Regulatory frameworks that ensure the stability and transparency of the global financial system.

  • Basel III: Requires banks to maintain high levels of capital and liquidity to withstand economic crises, ensuring their solvency.
  • AML and KYC: "Anti-Money Laundering" and "Know Your Customer" protocols to verify identity and prevent financial crimes.
  • SWIFT CSCF: A cybersecurity framework with 25 mandatory controls to protect all transactions conducted through the SWIFT network.

SWIFT Messages and Payments

Standardized and secure communications that form the backbone of international finance.

  • MT 799 (Pre-advice): A message confirming that funds are ready, sent before the formal transaction.
  • MT 103 (Transfer): Confirmation of a direct international bank transfer, ensuring that the payment has been completed.
  • MT 199 (Query): A free-format message used for clarifications or queries between banks, ensuring fluid communication.

Performance Bond

A guarantee from a bank or an insurance company to the client (*principal*) of a contract. It ensures that the contractor (*obligor*) will fulfill the obligations set forth in the contract.

  • **Purpose:** Protects the contracting party from economic losses if the supplier fails to meet their contractual obligations, such as delivery deadlines or product quality.
  • **Common Use:** It is frequently used in construction projects, large-scale goods supply contracts, and other transactions where contractual compliance is critical.

Escrow Account

A guarantee deposit account managed by an impartial third party.

  • The money or assets are held in this account until the conditions established by both parties in a contract are met.
  • Strategic Location: We offer the possibility of managing these accounts through banking partners in Portugal.

Payment Methods

  • 100% Payment at Origin Port: The full payment is made at the moment the goods are loaded at the port of departure.
  • 50% Payment at Origin Port and 50% at Destination Port: An initial payment is made upon loading the goods, and the balance is settled upon arrival at the destination port.
  • 100% Payment at Destination Port: The total payment is made when the goods arrive at their destination port.

*Note: Payment methods apply under certain conditions and are negotiable.
